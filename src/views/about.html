Our Story

Preserving Nigerian heritage through timeless fashion

From Lagos to the World

Founded in 2010 by Adeola Jade Johnson, Jade's Clothing began as a small boutique in Lagos, Nigeria.

What started as a passion project to showcase the beauty of Nigerian textiles has grown into an internationally recognized brand celebrating African heritage through fashion.

Our mission is to preserve traditional craftsmanship while creating contemporary styles that resonate with modern Africans and global fashion enthusiasts alike.

Adeola Jade Johnson
Founder & Creative Director

Our Journey

2010 - Humble Beginnings

Opened our first boutique in Lagos with just 20 original designs, all hand-sewn by local tailors.

2014 - First International Show

Showcased at London Fashion Week, introducing Nigerian textiles to global audiences.

2017 - E-commerce Launch

Launched our online store, making authentic Nigerian fashion accessible worldwide.

2020 - Artisan Collective

Established training programs supporting over 100 local artisans and tailors across Nigeria.

2023 - Global Recognition

Featured in Vogue Africa and named "Best Ethical Fashion Brand" at the African Fashion Awards.

Artisan Spotlight

Every Jade's garment tells a story of skilled craftsmanship.

We partner directly with Nigerian artisans who have preserved traditional textile techniques passed down through generations. From Adire dye masters in Abeokuta to loom weavers in Kano, each piece celebrates Nigeria's rich artisanal heritage.

Our Artisan Development Program provides training, fair wages, and international exposure for these talented craftspeople, ensuring these traditions continue to thrive.
